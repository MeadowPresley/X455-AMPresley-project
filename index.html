Bio

Meadow Presley is a practicing artist and credentialed teacher with over twenty years experience working with both youth and adults. Her teaching experience includes ceramics, mosaics, drawing and painting, watercolor, and mixed media/textiles. Art Mediums

Mosaics: Meadow Presley has designed and facilitated numerous collaborative community mosaics for Stepping Stone Growth Center, the San Leandro Improvement Agency, Peterson Cat, Kaiser Permanente, and Creative Growth Art Center. Her whimsical mixed media mosaic murals and panels include broken tile, handmade ceramic tiles, mirror, glass and found objects. Meadow taught mosaics in the Artful Steps program at Stepping Stones Growth Center in San Leandro for ten years.

Ceramics: Meadow Presley creates both functional and sculptural ceramic artwork around the themes of nature, primarily native California plants and animals, and nostalgia. Her ceramics have been exhibited in numerous Bay Area galleries and featured in East Bay Open Studios. Meadow has taught ceramics for Oakland Unified School District, Stepping Stones Growth Center in San Leandro and is currently teaching at Berkeley Adult School.

Watercolor: Meadow Presley began working extensively on her mixed media watercolor paintings during the pandemic in 2020. She combines her interests in California flora and fauna, botanical drawing, and nostalgia with watercolor techniques such as wet on wet, water soluble graphite and water color pencil. She currently teaches Watercolor Techniques at Berkeley Adult School in Berkeley, California.
